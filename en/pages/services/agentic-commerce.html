Shopping is shifting: instead of searching themselves, customers let their AI research, compare and order. For these agents a beautiful storefront counts for nothing – what counts is clean, machine-readable data and an interface they can dock onto.

The standards for this are only just emerging, which is why this service is marked as experimental. That is exactly why we go in small steps: we start with what already pays off today (findable, correctly understood product data) and open the checkout as soon as it makes sense for your case.

Isn’t this too early?

For fully automated buying: partly yes – the protocols are young. The groundwork pays off immediately though, because the same clean data already decides today whether AI searches find your products and represent them correctly.

Do I have to replace my shop?

No. Agentic commerce sits alongside your existing shop – same catalog, same prices, just additionally accessible to agents.

Which standards do you use?

Vendor-neutral, depending on the case: MCP for the agent API, ACP (ChatGPT Instant Checkout) and Google AP2 for buying, plus structured product data. Where nothing has settled yet, we build so that switching stays open.

Do I keep control over prices and orders?

Yes. Which products, which prices and which quantities an agent may see and order is up to you – with limits, approvals and a traceable log.

A real-world example: the SEO Agent continuously checks whether your pages are findable for Google and for AI search – the basis for agents considering your offer at all.

Why work with me?

Agentic commerce is new, and much of it is promise rather than practice. I separate the two: we implement what already holds up today – and prepare for what is clearly coming.
